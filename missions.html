Iconic Missions

These missions reshaped our understanding of the universe, pushing the limits of engineering and science.

Completed

Apollo 11

1969

The culmination of the space race, Apollo 11 fulfilled the goal of landing a human on the Moon and safely returning to Earth. The mission carried a plaque bearing the message: "We came in peace for all mankind."

Completed

Cassini-Huygens

1997 - 2017

An incredibly successful mission to Saturn. Cassini spent 13 years orbiting the ringed planet, while the Huygens probe achieved the first landing on a world in the outer solar system (Titan).

Active

Voyager Program

1977 - Present

Voyager 1 and 2 took advantage of a rare planetary alignment to explore the outer solar system. They carry the Golden Record and are humanity's farthest reaching spacecraft.

Active

Hubble Telescope

1990 - Present

Floating above Earth's atmospheric distortion, Hubble has revolutionized astronomy, helping pin down the age of the universe and capturing the breathtaking pillars of creation.

Active

Curiosity

2012 - Present

A car-sized rover designed to explore Gale Crater on Mars as part of NASA's Mars Science Laboratory mission. It determined that ancient Mars could have supported life.

Active

James Webb

2021 - Present

Operating in the infrared spectrum, JWST allows us to see through dust clouds and observe some of the very first galaxies ever formed, peering deep into the past.
